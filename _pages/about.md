**Welcome to my personal website.**

*I joined CREST (Finance group) from 1 Nov 2025 as a postdoc researcher in Paris.*

I am a fifth-year PhD candidate in Economics at Tilburg University. My research interests lie broadly within Trade, Environmental Economics, and Finance. My current research is in Climate Finance, where I draw on trade and macroeconomic theory to motivate and structure empirical tests. I am supervised by Prof. Harry Huizinga, Prof. Harald Benink, and Dr. Louis Raes.

My research is motivated by a broader question: how can capital markets contribute to addressing climate change as a global public good problem? In my dissertation, I examine how financial markets have structurally responded to climate change, with a particular focus on the international provision of brown financing and the allocation of control over brown firms. Across three chapters, I study three forms of cross-border capital flows: equity investment, corporate control through M&A, and bank lending. Together, these chapters document how climate-related financial risks are transferred internationally through capital markets.
